Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course the student will be able to:

  • Define style sheet language.

  • Describe the purpose of a cascading style sheet.

  • Describe the function of a cascading style sheet in the presentation of a webpage and website.

  • Describe how CSS2.0 and 3.0 enable multiple webpages to separate content from presentation.

  • Describe how CSS2.0 and 3.0 reduce the complexity and repetition in structural content and its presentation

  • Apply CSS in the creation of webpages and websites

  • Classify ‘selectors’

  • Create CSS classes

  • Apply CSS classes to DIV tags

  • Create layered web sites using DIV tags

  • Create inline CSS, internal and external CSS files

Statement on Classroom Policies

Attendance Policies

Students are expected to attend each class and arrive promptly in order to do satisfactory work and not disturb other learners. Attendance will be taken at least once during each class. If you miss more than three (3) classes during the course you will NOT be allowed to pass the course until after the instructor receives notification from the Dean(SEA) that the student will be allowed to receive your grade. DO NOT EMAIL YOUR INSTRUCTOR FOR ANY GRADES.

Students are responsible for all assignments, papers, and examinations to be submitted on time, even when you are ill or absent from class. It is THE STUDENT’S RESPONSIBILITY to ensure that they obtain copies of missed work, lectures, and assignments from fellow students if a class is missed. The instructor may make available lectures for downloading to a flash drive during class breaks, on the Website or by email.

Arrangements for completion of all assigned work should be made prior to planned absences, except with unforeseen emergencies. Leave a message with the receptionist if you are planning to be absent and for emergencies ONLY. Any other queries will be done by email. See the instructor for the definition of ‘emergency’.

No Make-up Examinations

Typically, any missed assignments will result in a deduction of one full grade point PER DAY. The midterm and final examinations must be taken by learners on the scheduled day and time as there is no guarantee a make-up examination can be arranged. Alternative forms of examinations will NOT be considered for students who do not like the examination format in this course. Also, only in emergencies will the instructor allow for a deviation in the examination schedule, or grading of late assignments, for individual students. Plan holidays and trips to occur AFTER the final examination.

Statement on Plagiarism

Plagiarism is a form of cheating and is a very serious academic offence that may lead to expulsion from your program. “Plagiarized material” is any material that is drawn from and presented in written, graphic and visual form, including electronic data and oral presentations. Plagiarism occurs when the origin of the material used in not properly cited. If you are unsure about plagiarism, or if you have any questions, please speak with your Instructor or Dean(SEA).
